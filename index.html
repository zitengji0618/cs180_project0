I think it is because of perspective, when shooting very close with a wide lens, parts of the face that are nearer to the camera (nose, forehead) are proportionally much closer than the ears, so they look enlarged. If you step back and zoom to keep the same framing, the relative distance differences (for example nose vs. ears/cheeks) shrink dramatically, so features appear flatter and more natural.
